But somehow (and thankfully) she wasn’t. Truthfully, I met Kate a while before this day. I honestly can’t remember when, but I know (as does she) that I did not like her. It had nothing to do with her personality and even nothing to do with her at all. Like all of my other thoughts and feelings, it was highly unreasonable. I didn’t like her because my freshman year for some reason people loved me. And it felt really nice. But then my sophomore year Kate joined and Hannah Entwistle would keep saying “she’s the next Alex!” and I felt like I was being replaced. So, like a bratty, spoiled only child whose younger sibling was just born, I decided that I did not like Kate. But of course, Kaitlyn Elizabeth Przydzial is an absolute angel of a rainbow child whom no one could ever hate, and so I soon came to very much like her. And then the sleepover before kickoff happened. Not a single freshman came. Except Kate. Lovely Mr. and Mrs. Przydzial came to drop her off and were so amazing (as they are). And that night I knew, and we all knew, that she was in for the long run. Because if that didn’t scare her away, absolutely nothing could. Seriously. Let’s go through a runthrough of the night, shall we? Or actually, to keep this short (because there are so many more parts ahead) I’ll just say one word: teeth. Just kidding!
